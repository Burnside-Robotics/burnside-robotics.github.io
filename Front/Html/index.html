ABOUT US

Robotics is a club where students can invent, learn and develop ideas with others in a fun, safe environment. In robotics, over the course of a year, teams of students will develop a robot which will be competed against other robots in games. This robot will be programmed and built with Vex parts like aluminium parts or Lego-like parts, this teaches students how to use tools and build to make a functional technological system. Once the robot is completed, the teams will then travel to Auckland or Palmerston North to compete in the annual Vex Nationals. The Nationals is a time where students will see if their robot fails or succeeds. Students will learn different ways of solving problems and will meet friends all around the country and others like themselves. They will also learn vital STEM skills such as building, programming and teamwork skills which can help them in STEM fields and in everyday life.

WHAT WE OFFER

At robotics, we offer Vex VRC and Vex IQ. Vex VRC is the category in which robots are built out of metal parts and compete with other robots. Vex IQ is the category in which robots are built out of Lego-like pieces and play alongside other robots to attain the highest score. The teams with the highest scores go to Worlds and in 2020, BHS X got the opportunity to go to Worlds. The Robotics club allows for any student who is willing to learn to be able to develop various skills in STEM. Skills such as problem solving, programming, building and working in a team. This helps students to think outside the box and will help them in the future. Joining robotics will not only help students develop these important skills and attain knowledge but it will also help them create friends and try new things in this safe environment.

WHAT IS VEX?

Vex robotics is the fastest growing robotics competition in the world. It contains three divisions: Vex VRC, Vex IQ, and Vex U. Burnside Robotics compete in the Vex VRC division which is for intermediate and high school students. This year we are planning to compete in Vex IQ (which also has the same divisions) as this year we have enough teams to be able to explore that field.

Vex VRC has an annual game for each of its divisions and these games are introduced in April and are completed in NZ in February. Vex IQ also has an annual game for each of its divisions except these games are introduced in April and are completed in NZ in December. This allows competitors to always be kept on their toes and helps them to think of solutions in different ways. This means students need to design, build and program a robot for each new challenge every year.

Vex IQ

Vex IQ is also a robotics system designed for years 4-10. It uses mostly lego like parts and electric motors. Vex IQ robots are significantly smaller but still carry out the same principals. For the competition they still have a size limit, motor limit and the type of components used are also limited. These restrictions are what pushes students to think outside the box. Although using lego this means the structure is more fragile, students have to think of ways to make it work and that is what makes this challenge a challenge.
