ABOUT US

Robotics is a club where students can invent, learn and develop ideas with others in a fun, safe environment. In robotics, over the course of a year, teams of students will develop a robot which will be competed against other robots in games. This robot will be programmed and built with Vex parts like aluminium parts or Lego-like parts, this teaches students how to use tools and build to make a functional technological system. Once the robot is completed, the teams will then travel to Auckland or Palmerston North to compete in the annual Vex Nationals. The Nationals is a time where students will see if their robot fails or succeeds. Students will learn different ways of solving problems and will meet friends all around the country and others like themselves.

WHAT WE OFFER

At robotics, we offer Vex VRC and Vex IQ. Vex VRC is the category in which robots are built out of metal parts and compete with other robots. Vex IQ is the category in which robots are built out of Lego-like pieces and play with other robots to attain the highest score. The teams with the highest scores go to Worlds and in 2020, BHS X got the opportunity to go to Worlds. The Robotics club allows for any student who is willing to learn to be able to develop various skills in STEM. Skills such as problem solving, programming, building and working in a team. This helps students to think outside the box and will help them in the future. Joining robotics will not only help students develop these important skills and attain knowledge but it will also help them create friends and try new things in this safe environment.

WHAT IS VEX?

Vex Robotics is the fast growing robotics competition in the world. It contains three divisions: Vex EDR, Vex IQ, and Vex U. Burnside Robotics compete in the Vex EDR division which is for Intermediate and High School students. This year we are planning to compete in Vex IQ as we have enough teams to be able to explore that field. Vex has an annual game for each of its division and these games are introduced in April and are competed in February. This allows competitors to always be kept on their toes and helps them to think of solutions in different ways. This means students need to design, build and program a robot for each new challenge.
